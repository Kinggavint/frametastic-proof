Our Story

It Started With a Piece of Wood and a Vision

Frametastic was born from a simple truth: mass-produced frames can never do justice to something meaningful. What started as a personal project — building a custom frame for a family photograph — quickly became a passion, then a craft, then a calling.

Working out of a small workshop in Provo, Utah, surrounded by the Wasatch Mountains, we began crafting frames that weren't just functional but expressive. Each one hand-selected from quality hardwoods, shaped with traditional tools, and finished with techniques passed down through generations of woodworkers.

"I don't just build frames — I build the first thing people see when they look at something they love."

Over the years, the craft expanded beyond wood. We learned metalwork — hand-hammering copper and forging iron brackets. We explored painted finishes that bring warmth and personality. We developed mixed-media techniques that combine materials in unexpected ways.

Our Philosophy

What Guides Every Frame We Build

Time is an Ingredient

Good things can't be rushed. We take the time each piece demands — whether that's hours of careful engraving or days of waiting for a finish to cure naturally.

Made With Intention

Every frame starts with a conversation about what it will hold. We design around the story — the art, the memory, the moment — not around a catalog number.

Layers of Quality

From the raw material selection to the final protective seal, every layer matters. We source locally when possible and never cut corners on what you can't see.

Every Frame Tells a Story

There's a reason you chose that particular photograph, that painting, that print. It means something. Maybe it's the way the light fell on a mountain morning. Maybe it's the face of someone you love. Maybe it's a piece of art that stopped you in your tracks at a gallery.

Whatever it is, it deserves more than a mass-produced rectangle from a big-box store. It deserves something built by hand — something that complements the story already being told and adds a new layer of meaning.

That's what Frametastic is about. Not just framing — but honoring. Building something that makes you pause, lean in, and appreciate the art and the frame in equal measure.
